# Launch content blueprint

Status: working copy for design and development. Statements labeled **draft** or **pending confirmation** must not be treated as approved client claims.

## Messaging hierarchy

### Primary promise

**Make mismatched masonry belong.**

This is the clearest expression of the verified core service: color staining and matching repairs and additions to existing masonry.

### Supporting explanation

Masonry Color Corrections LLC selectively adjusts the color of brick and other absorbent masonry so repairs, additions, and visibly different areas blend more naturally with the surrounding work.

“Other absorbent masonry” stays deliberately conservative until the complete material list is confirmed.

### Primary actions

- **Request an estimate**
- **Call (513) 612-8421**

### Draft brand voice

- Specific rather than promotional.
- Confident without promising an invisible or perfect match.
- Plainspoken enough for homeowners, credible enough for masons and builders.
- Focused on the observed problem, the matching judgment, and the finished relationship between old and new.
- Never call stain “paint.”
- Never claim permanence, a lifetime warranty, certification, VOC status, or maintenance-free performance without documentation.

### Stain versus paint section

Heading: **Change the color without hiding the masonry.**

Draft body:

> Paint forms a film across the surface and can conceal the natural variation and texture that make masonry look like masonry. The color-correction approach used by MCC is intended to adjust color while preserving the visual character of the material. The exact technical explanation will be finalized after the stain system and preparation process are confirmed.

This wording intentionally avoids unsupported durability, moisture, chemical-bond, breathability, and warranty claims.

### Process section

Heading: **A careful match starts before the first color is applied.**

Draft steps, pending operational confirmation:

1. **Share the problem.** Send photos, location, material, and the result you are trying to achieve.
2. **Assess the masonry.** MCC determines whether color correction is appropriate and what conditions may affect the work.
3. **Establish the target.** The surrounding masonry, texture, variation, and normal viewing conditions guide the match.
4. **Correct and refine.** Color is built carefully so the adjusted area relates naturally to the original work.
5. **Review the result.** The completed work is assessed as part of the full wall or structure, not only at arm’s length.

CTA: **Request an estimate**
