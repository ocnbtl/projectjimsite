# Client intake and asset checklist

Use this as the next client conversation. Questions marked **Critical** affect the site’s public truth, legal identity, conversion behavior, or launch readiness.

## Confirmed before intake

- The older **Masonry Color Restoration, LLC** BBB profile belongs to the same business.
- The current website and public brand should use **Masonry Color Corrections LLC**, abbreviated **MCC** where useful.
- The BBB profile contains an older name and address and appears not to have been updated recently.

## 1. Business identity and contact

- **Confirmed:** Use Masonry Color Corrections LLC as the current public name.
- **Critical:** What is the exact legal/DBA history, and may the website say “serving the area since 2013” based on the older BBB profile?
- **Critical:** May the website link to the BBB profile or show its current rating while the listing still carries the older name/address?
- **Critical:** What is the canonical phone number and monitored email address for website leads?
- **Critical:** Should 7484 Victoria Falls Ave, Maineville, OH be shown publicly, or is the business a service-area business whose street address should remain hidden?
- What customer-facing hours should appear?
- Is texting the business number encouraged?
- Who should receive estimate notifications and backup alerts?
- What response time can the website honestly promise?

## 2. Owner story and trust

- **Critical:** Who owns the business, and what name/title may be published?
- When did the current business begin?
- How many years of relevant masonry/color-matching experience can be documented?
- Is the company insured, bonded, licensed, certified, veteran-owned, family-owned, or a member of any professional organization?
- Which credentials may be shown, and can source documents or badge-use permissions be provided?
- What makes the company’s approach meaningfully different from a general mason or painter?
- What kinds of clients refer work to the business?

## 4. Product, process, and claims

- **Critical:** What stain manufacturer/system does the company use?
- Is the company certified by that manufacturer? If yes, provide current proof and logo-use rules.
- What surfaces can the actual system stain?
- What makes a surface unsuitable, including paint, sealers, low absorption, damage, or moisture?
- Does the company perform a water/absorption test?
- How are colors sampled and approved?
- Is stain mixed on site or in advance?
- Is each brick treated individually, or are larger surfaces sprayed/rolled/brushed?
- What preparation and masking occur?
- What weather and temperature limits apply?
- What is the real step-by-step customer process from first contact through final inspection?
- How long do common projects take?
- What maintenance is required?
- **Critical:** Is there a written labor or product warranty? Provide the exact terms.
- Which claims can be documented: breathable, penetrating, mineral-based, VOC-free, fade resistant, maintenance-free, permanent, no peeling/flaking, or environmentally safer?
- What should customers understand about stain versus paint?
- What results or perfect-match promises should the site avoid?

## 5. Service area

- **Critical:** What is the real travel radius?
- Which counties are primary?
- Which cities or communities should be named because the company actively serves them?
- Does the company cross into Northern Kentucky or Indiana?
- Are distant commercial projects handled differently?
- Are there trip charges or minimum project sizes outside the core area?

## 6. Project photography and case studies

- **Critical:** Provide original, highest-resolution project files rather than Facebook downloads.
- Pair each before image with the matching after image.
- Identify whether each image is before, sample/testing, in progress, or after.
- Confirm the business owns the images or has permission to publish them.
- Remove or flag addresses, faces, license plates, house numbers, or other private details.
- For every candidate project, provide:
  - General location.
  - Residential/commercial category.
  - Material.
  - Customer problem.
  - Scope of correction.
  - Product/system used.
  - Approximate duration.
  - Any unusual constraints.
  - Result and customer feedback.
- Identify the five strongest transformations for launch.
- Identify at least one repair/addition blend, one larger stain transformation, and one interior/fireplace or stone example if offered.
- If possible, take new wide, medium, and detail photos in consistent daylight.

## 7. Reviews and reputation

- Where do current reviews live: Google, Facebook, BBB, Angi, Houzz, email, text, or another source?
- Which testimonials may be republished?
- Provide the exact text, reviewer display name, source URL or screenshot, project type, and permission status.
- Are there builders, masons, architects, or property managers willing to provide a professional endorsement?
- Are there any complaints or recurring objections the website should address directly?

## 8. Branding

- **Critical:** Provide the original logo file in SVG, AI, EPS, PDF, or the highest-resolution transparent PNG available.
- Was the Facebook logo graphic generated for the business, and are there unrestricted commercial rights to it?
- Is the “MCC” monogram important to preserve?
- Are gray and red required brand colors or open to refinement?
- Are there vehicle graphics, yard signs, uniforms, business cards, invoices, or other materials the website should align with?
- What should the brand never look or sound like?
- Is a refreshed production logo within scope if a clean source does not exist?

## 9. Estimate and sales process

- **Critical:** What information is required before deciding whether a project is a fit?
- Are photos required before scheduling a visit?
- Which file types and maximum upload size are practical?
- Is an on-site estimate free?
- Can some projects be estimated from photos?
- Is there a minimum charge?
- How far out is the normal schedule?
- What happens after form submission?
- Should leads be called, texted, or emailed first?
- Should the form ask whether the visitor is a homeowner, contractor, architect, property manager, or insurer?
- What areas or project types should be filtered out politely?
- What CRM, inbox, spreadsheet, or other system should store leads?

## 10. Domain, accounts, and launch access

- **Critical:** What domain is owned or preferred?
- Who controls the registrar and DNS?
- Is there an existing Google Business Profile? Who has owner access?
- Is there an existing Google Analytics, Search Console, or Tag Manager property?
- Which Facebook/Instagram/other social accounts should be linked?
- Who should own the GitHub repository, deployment account, analytics, and form service?
- Who gives final approval for copy, design, privacy language, and launch?

## 11. Policies and privacy

- Will the website collect project addresses and customer photos?
- How long should submitted files and lead data be retained?
- Who may access submitted information?
- Should marketing consent be separate from estimate contact consent?
- Is call tracking planned?
- Are analytics or advertising cookies planned?
- Provide any existing privacy policy, terms, warranty, or estimate disclaimer.

## Minimum asset package to unlock design and copy

1. Confirmed public business name and NAP.
2. Confirmed service matrix and service area.
3. Actual stain system, process, certification, warranty, and allowed claims.
4. Owner biography and trust facts.
5. Five launch-quality project stories with paired original images.
6. Three or more publishable reviews if available.
7. Production logo source or approval to refresh it.
8. Domain preference and lead-routing decision.
