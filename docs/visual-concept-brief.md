# Visual concept brief

Status: design-direction source for the first production concept.

## Human, task, and feeling

The visitor is usually standing near a repair, addition, fireplace, or exterior wall that keeps catching their eye. They may have just finished a costly repair and are frustrated that the new work still looks wrong. They need to decide whether MCC understands the mismatch, has credible proof, and is easy to contact.

The site should feel like a careful material sample room: grounded, exacting, tactile, quiet, and reassuring. Premium means judgment and restraint, not luxury decoration.

## Domain exploration

- Brick bond and running courses.
- Mortar joints and reveals.
- Sample panels and color swatches.
- Mineral pigment, fired clay, lime, soot, and weathering.
- Old/new adjacency.
- Normal viewing distance versus close inspection.
- Selective correction and gradual blending.
- Masking, edge control, and unit-by-unit attention.
- Before/after alignment.

## Color world

- Fired-clay red.
- Deep iron oxide.
- Limestone and warm mortar.
- Soot charcoal.
- Weathered umber.
- Buff brick.
- Muted sage/lichen as a restrained secondary material note.

## Signature element

**The correction seam:** a recurring offset vertical joint that divides “before” from “after,” then reappears as the page’s alignment system. It should appear in the hero comparison, section transitions, case-study media, process numbering, and footer geometry. It must feel derived from a masonry joint rather than a generic split-screen slider.

## Defaults to reject

1. Generic contractor hero with a worker and dark overlay → a close, honest before/after masonry field with the correction seam and no tinted photo wash.
2. Repeated icon-card service grid → a running-course editorial layout with three differently proportioned service bands.
3. Black-and-gold “premium trades” styling → mineral off-white, soot, clay, buff, and restrained oxide colors taken from actual masonry.
4. Unlabeled gallery tiles → case-study spreads with problem, material, intervention, and result.
5. Software-like pills and fake browser frames → one strong architectural frame and quiet utility details.

## Visual system

- Background character: warm limestone, not pure white and not yellow cream.
- Primary text: soot charcoal.
- Accent: fired-clay red used for action and the correction seam.
- Secondary surfaces: buff and pale mortar shifts.
- Depth: subtle surface-color shifts with very light borders; no dramatic shadows.
- Radius: medium architectural rounding on the primary frame, smaller controlled rounding elsewhere.
- Typography: a confident, compact grotesk for display and navigation paired with a highly readable humanist sans for body copy. Avoid overly geometric SaaS fonts and decorative slab serifs.
- Spacing: generous 8px-based rhythm with tight alignment at masonry-derived increments.
- Imagery: real project photography ultimately; concept imagery may use close material studies but must never masquerade as client work.
- Icons: minimal, monoline, only when they clarify call, estimate, navigation, or process direction.
- Motion: the correction seam reveals change deliberately; section content uses restrained fade/translate with reduced-motion fallback.

## Homepage concept sections

1. Quiet header and first viewport with MCC wordmark, essential navigation, one estimate button, outcome-led copy, and a dominant before/after material frame.
2. Problem-recognition and service pathways using running-course proportions rather than equal cards.
3. Signature case-study spread and stain-versus-paint education with macro material detail.
4. Process sequence, service-area cue, photo-first estimate panel, and substantial local footer.
